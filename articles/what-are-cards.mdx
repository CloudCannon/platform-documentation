A Card is a square, user interface element used to represent your data in CloudCannon. Cards are clickable elements that preview the data they represent.

CloudCannon uses Cards to represent your data in many places across the app, including:

* Files in your [Collection](/documentation/articles/define-your-collections/) browser (but not the File browser)
* The [Object](/documentation/articles/object-inputs/), [Array](/documentation/articles/array-inputs/), and [Select and Multiselect](/documentation/articles/select-and-multiselect-inputs/) inputs
* [Structures](/documentation/articles/what-is-a-structure/), including the [Structures modal](/documentation/articles/configure-your-structure-previews/)
* [Snippets](/documentation/articles/what-is-a-snippet/), including the Snippets modal

<comp.DocsImage path="https://cc-dam.imgix.net/documentation/images/editing/cards/2024-08/CloudCannon-Documentation-Collection-Browser-File-Cards.png" alt="A screenshot of the Collection browser showing four cards to represent four files." title="The Collection browser" type="ui-snippet"/>

You can [configure the appearance of your Cards](/documentation/articles/configure-your-card-previews/) to customize a better experience for your team members, and specify a different appearance for each data type (e.g., Collections may preview their Collection items using different Card configurations).

Cards have five main elements: the text, subtext, metadata, icon, and image.

<comp.DocsImage path="https://cc-dam.imgix.net/documentation/images/editing/cards/2024-08/CloudCannon-Documentation-Card-Preview-Elements.png" alt="A diagram of a Card in CloudCannon, showing all the configurable elements." title="The components of a Card" type="ui-snippet"/>

The text, subtext, and metadata on a Card describe the data. The text normally displays the title of the data, using the first nested value (prioritizing text values) by default. Subtext

&nbsp;

The icon from Google's [Material Icons](https://fonts.google.com/icons?icon.set=Material+Icons) list.

The image

The gallery space on a Card can display text, an icon, or an image. Most commonly, these are used by file Cards to display a preview of your webpage. Some Cards, such as those used in [Inputs](/documentation/articles/what-are-inputs/), do not have a gallery space. Additionally, you can choose to disable the gallery space if you do not require it.

You can configure the appearance of your Cards using the `previews` key. For more information, please read our documentation on [configuring your Card previews](/documentation/articles/configure-your-card-previews/) or our documentation specific to each application (i.e., [Collections](/documentation/articles/define-your-collections/), [Inputs](/documentation/articles/what-are-inputs/), [Structures](/documentation/articles/what-is-a-structure/), and [Snippets](/documentation/articles/what-is-a-snippet/)).

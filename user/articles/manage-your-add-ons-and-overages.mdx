<comp.Notice info_type="permissions">
  <p><strong>Permissions required</strong></p>
  <p>Members of the <em>Owners</em> or <em>Billing</em> <a href="/documentation/user-articles/what-are-default-permission-groups/">Default Permission Groups</a>, or <a href="/documentation/user-articles/what-are-custom-permission-groups/">Custom Permission Groups</a> with the <code>org:billing:write</code> permission, can manage add-ons.</p>
</comp.Notice>

<comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/a/add-on.yml">Add-ons</comp.GlossaryTerm> allow you to extend the resource limits included in your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/s/subscription.yml">Subscription</comp.GlossaryTerm> plan by purchasing additional capacity in bundles. When <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/o/overage.yml">overages</comp.GlossaryTerm> are enabled, CloudCannon automatically purchases add-on bundles when your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/o/organization.yml">Organization</comp.GlossaryTerm> exceeds its plan limits.

For more information about available add-on types and how overage charges work, please read the documentation on [add-ons and overages](/documentation/user-articles/what-are-add-ons/).

<comp.DocShot docshot_key="CloudCannon-Documentation-Your-Subscription-Plan-Annual-Standard-Plan" alt="A screenshot of the Your Subscription Plan page shows the Hosting add-ons toggle, plan resources, payment details, and estimated bill." title="The Your Subscription Plan page" type="screenshot"/>

To enable or disable overages:

1. Navigate to the *Subscription* page under *Org Settings* by clicking on the *Org Settings* button in the *App Sidebar*.
2. Click the *Review your billing plan* link in the billing summary. CloudCannon will navigate you to the *Your Subscription Plan* page.
3. Switch the *Hosting add-ons* toggle ON or OFF.
4. Click the *Enable/Disable add-ons* button.

CloudCannon will update your overage settings immediately.

<comp.Notice info_type="important">
  Disabling overages means your *Sites* may go offline if you exceed your bandwidth limit. Consider your usage before disabling this setting.
</comp.Notice>

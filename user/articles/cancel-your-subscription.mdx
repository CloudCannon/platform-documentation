<comp.Notice info_type="permissions">
  <p><strong>Permissions required</strong></p>
  <p>Members of the <em>Owners</em> or <em>Billing</em> <a href="/documentation/user-articles/what-are-default-permission-groups/">Default Permission Groups</a>, or <a href="/documentation/user-articles/what-are-custom-permission-groups/">Custom Permission Groups</a> with the <code>org:billing:write</code> permission, can cancel a subscription.</p>
</comp.Notice>

You can cancel your paid CloudCannon *Subscription* at any time by downgrading to the free Personal plan. Cancellation does not delete your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/o/organization.yml">Organization</comp.GlossaryTerm>, *Sites*, or files — it reduces your access to features and resource limits.

When you downgrade to the Personal plan:

* Your *Sites* and files remain intact, but features available only on paid plans will no longer be accessible.
* Resource limits are reduced to Personal plan levels (limited build time, bandwidth, users, and custom domains).
* If your usage exceeds the Personal plan's limits at the time of cancellation, you may need to remove resources before the downgrade takes effect.

## Downgrade from the *Subscription* page

<comp.DocShot docshot_key="CloudCannon-Documentation-Update-Your-Plan-Monthly-Comparison" alt="A screenshot of the plan selection page shows the available CloudCannon plans with pricing and feature details." title="The Update your Plan page" type="screenshot"/>

To downgrade to the Personal plan from the *Subscription* page:

1. Navigate to the *Subscription* page under *Org Settings* by clicking *Org Settings* in the *App Sidebar*.
2. Click the *Review your billing plan* link in the billing summary. CloudCannon will navigate you to the *Your Subscription Plan* page.
3. Click the *Change your plan* button. CloudCannon will navigate you to the *Update your plan* page.
4. Select *Personal* at the bottom of the plan selection page.
5. If your current usage exceeds the Personal plan's limits, CloudCannon will open the *Confirm resources downgrade* modal. Review which resources exceed the new limits and follow the provided links to resolve them (e.g., removing team members or custom domains).
6. Click *Confirm downgrade*.

CloudCannon will downgrade your *Organization* to the Personal plan. You will not receive any further invoices for your paid plan, and any remaining time on your previous billing period will be credited to your account.

## Downgrade from the *Delete* page

You can also downgrade to the Personal plan from the *Delete* page in *Org Settings*. If you are considering deleting your *Organization*, CloudCannon provides the option to downgrade instead, allowing you to keep your *Sites* and files.

<comp.DocShot docshot_key="CloudCannon-Documentation-Org-Settings-Delete" alt="A screenshot of the Delete page in Org Settings shows the option to downgrade to the Personal plan." title="The Delete page" type="screenshot"/>

To downgrade from the *Delete* page:

1. Navigate to the *Delete* page under *Org Settings* by clicking *Org Settings* in the *App Sidebar*, then selecting *Delete*.
2. Click *Downgrade to Personal plan*. CloudCannon will navigate you to the *Your Subscription Plan* page.
3. Click *Cancel my subscription*.
4. If your current usage exceeds the Personal plan's limits, CloudCannon will open the *Resource limit exceeded* modal. Review which resources are over the new limits and follow the provided links to resolve them.
5. Click *Confirm downgrade*.

<comp.DocShot docshot_key="CloudCannon-Documentation-Downgrade-Plan-Modal-Resource-Limit-Exceeded" alt="A screenshot of the Resource limit exceeded modal shows the resources that exceed the Personal plan's limits." title="The Resource limit exceeded modal" type="ui-snippet"/>

CloudCannon will downgrade your *Organization* to the Personal plan.

<comp.Notice info_type="info">
  Cancellation is different from a *Subscription* pause. A pause happens automatically when CloudCannon cannot collect payment after multiple retry attempts. For more information, please read the documentation on [how CloudCannon handles failed payments](/documentation/user-articles/how-cloudcannon-handles-failed-payments/).
</comp.Notice>

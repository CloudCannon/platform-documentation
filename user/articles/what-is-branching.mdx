*Branching* is the process of copying information (e.g., content, code, and CloudCannon settings) from an existing <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/s/site.yml">Site</comp.GlossaryTerm> to a new *Site*. Each branched *Site* is independent, so you and your *Team Members* can work on different tasks without affecting each other's work, and without affecting your live website. *Site* branching is a <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/p/project.yml">Project</comp.GlossaryTerm> feature. To branch a *Site*, your developer needs to have [created a Project](/documentation/developer-articles/create-a-project/) for your website.

Every branched *Site* you make is stored in the <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/g/git-repository.yml">Git Repository</comp.GlossaryTerm> managed by your developer. CloudCannon handles communicating with this repository for you; you don't need access to your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/g/git-provider.yml">Git Provider</comp.GlossaryTerm> to branch a *Site* or edit and save content.

You can create a copy of your website at any time, without help from your developer, by branching a *Site* in your *Project*. The starting point for new branches is set by your developer when they configure the *Project*. By default, you can create branched *Sites* from the <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/m/main-branch.yml">Main Branch</comp.GlossaryTerm> selected by your developer when they created the *Project*. This is the primary version of your website, and typically the version live on your public website for visitors. However, if the *Allow all branching* setting is enabled, you can also create a branched *Site* from any *Site* in your *Project*. For more information, please read our documentation on [creating a branched Site](/documentation/user-articles/create-a-branched-site/).

A branched *Site* is like any other *Site* in CloudCannon. Any changes you make on a branched *Site* stay separate from any other *Site* until you are ready to publish them. You can view your changes on the branched *Site's* dedicated [Testing Domain](/documentation/user-articles/what-is-a-testing-domain/) and, when you are ready, publish those changes back to the branched *Site's* <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/p/publish-branch.yml">Publish Branch</comp.GlossaryTerm>. For more information, please read our documentation on [what publishing is](/documentation/user-articles/what-is-publishing/).

Let's walk through an example of branching.

<comp.DocsImage path="assets/diagrams/CloudCannon-Documentation-Branching-Workflow-Diagram.png" alt="A diagram of a branching workflow shows three project branches publish to the Staging Branch, which publishes to the Live Website branch." type="ui-snippet" />

In the diagram above, there are five *Sites*. "Production" is the live version of your website; the other four are branched *Sites*.

"Staging" is a branched copy of "Production". The three *Sites* below — "Write a new blog", "Remove a product page", and "Update Site navigation" — are each branched from "Staging". Different *Team Members* can work on each one independently without affecting the others or the live website.

A branched *Site* starts as an exact copy of the *Site* it was branched from. As you make changes on a branched *Site*, only the parts you and your *Team Members* have intentionally changed will diverge. The majority of the *Site* will still be the same. When you are happy with your changes, you can publish them back to the *Site* you branched from.

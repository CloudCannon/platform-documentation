<comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/a/add-on.yml">Add-ons</comp.GlossaryTerm> are extra capacity you can purchase in bundles to extend the limits included in your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/s/subscription.yml">Subscription</comp.GlossaryTerm> plan. Every CloudCannon plan includes a set amount of users, bandwidth, custom domains, and build time. If your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/o/organization.yml">Organization</comp.GlossaryTerm> needs more than your plan provides, add-ons let you increase those limits without upgrading to a higher plan.

<comp.DocShot docshot_key="CloudCannon-Documentation-Org-Settings-Subscription-Annual-Standard-Plan" alt="A screenshot of the Subscription page shows the billing summary, including the current plan, estimated bill, overages, and billing details." title="The Subscription page in Organization Settings" type="screenshot"/>

## Available add-ons

CloudCannon offers four types of add-ons:

* **Extra users** — Additional users for your *Organization*, including *Client Sharing* seats. Each add-on provides one extra user.
* **Extra bandwidth** — Additional hosting bandwidth for your *Sites*, purchased in fixed-size packages.
* **Extra custom domains** — Additional custom domains or subdomains for your *Sites*.
* **Extra build time** — Additional build hours for your *Sites*. Plans with unlimited build time (Standard and above) do not need this add-on.

The cost and bundle size of each add-on depends on your current plan. For up-to-date pricing, please see the [pricing page](/pricing/).

Add-on charges appear as individual line items on your invoices with descriptions such as:

* "Extra users"
* "Extra hosting bandwidth"
* "Custom domains"
* "Extra build time"

If you are on an annual *Subscription*, your base plan is billed annually, but add-on overages are billed monthly. Your billing summary shows these monthly overages in a separate section.

For more information on reading your invoices, please read the documentation on [accessing and configuring your invoices](/documentation/user-articles/access-and-configure-your-invoices/).

## Enabling or disabling overages

<comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/o/overage.yml">Overages</comp.GlossaryTerm> control what happens when your *Organization* exceeds a plan's included limits.

If you have overages enabled for your *Organization*, CloudCannon will automatically purchases add-on bundles when you exceed your plan's limits. These charges appear as line items on your next invoice.

If you have overages disabled for your *Organization*, you are limited to the ammounts specified in your CloudCannon *Subscription* plan.

* **Bandwidth limit exceeded** — Your *Sites* will be disabled and taken offline until the next billing period or until you enable overages.
* **Build time limit exceeded** — You will not be able to save changes that trigger a build until the next billing period or until you enable overages.

To enable or disable overages, please read the documentation on [managing your add-ons](/documentation/user-articles/manage-your-add-ons/).

## Track your bandwidth and build time

CloudCannon provides usage graphs for bandwidth and build time so you can monitor consumption against your plan limits. These graphs display cumulative usage over the current billing period and show your plan's included limit, making it easy to see whether you are approaching a threshold that would trigger an overage.

<comp.DocShot docshot_key="CloudCannon-Documentation-Site-Dashboard-Usage-Tab" alt="A screenshot of the Usage tab on the Site Dashboard shows the Bandwidth and Build Time graphs and tables." title="The Site Dashboard Usage tab" type="screenshot"/>

You can find usage graphs in two places:

* The *Usage* tab on your *Organization Home* page shows bandwidth and build time graphs for all *Sites* in you *Organization*. You can use the graph to get a overview of usage trends, or use the table to see which *Site* or URL is affecting your billing the most.
* The *Usage* tab on your *Site Dashboard* also shows bandwidth and build time graphs. The Bandwith graph and table show which URLs (or files) in a *Site* affect your billing the most, while the Build graph shows the build time is for one *Site* over time.

Monitoring these graphs regularly helps you anticipate when your *Organization* might exceed its plan limits and decide whether to purchase add-ons, enable overages, or upgrade your plan.

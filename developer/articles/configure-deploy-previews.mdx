<comp.Notice info_type="permissions">
  **Permissions required**

  Members of the Owners and Developers [Default Permission Groups](/documentation/user-articles/what-are-default-permission-groups/), or [Custom Permission Groups](/documentation/developer-articles/what-are-custom-permission-groups/) with the `project:settings:deploy-previews:write` permission, can configure *Deploy Previews* for a *Project*.
</comp.Notice>

<comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/d/deploy-preview.yml">Deploy Previews</comp.GlossaryTerm> are a set of automations that CloudCannon can run whenever a <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/p/pull-request.yml">Pull Request</comp.GlossaryTerm> is opened, closed, merged, or updated in your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/g/git-repository.yml">Git Repository</comp.GlossaryTerm>. Specifically, CloudCannon can automatically create *Sites* for each *Pull Request*, delete those *Sites* when a *Pull Request* is merged or closed, and post *Automatic Comments* on *Pull Requests* in your *Git provider* summarizing *Site* details in CloudCannon.

Before we begin, this article assumes you have already [created a Project](/documentation/developer-articles/create-a-project/) and that your *Organization* is on a plan that includes *Deploy Previews*.

To configure the *Deploy Preview* settings for a *Project*:

1. Click the *Projects* link in the *App Sidebar*. CloudCannon will open the *Projects Browser*.
2. Select the *Project* you want to configure. CloudCannon will open the *Project Sites Browser*.
3. Click the *Project Settings* tab.
4. Select *Deploy Previews* from the *Section Navigation*.
5. Use the checkboxes under the *Automatic Sites* and *Automatic Comments* headings to control which automations are enabled. Nested checkboxes (such as *Skip for draft Pull Requests*) only become available once their parent checkbox is enabled.
6. (Optional.) Add Markdown to the *Comment footer* text field.
7. Click the *Update Deploy Preview Settings* button at the bottom of the page.

CloudCannon will update your *Deploy Preview* settings.

<comp.DocShot docshot_key="CloudCannon-Documentation-Project-Settings-Deploy-Previews" alt="A screenshot of the Deploy Previews section in Project Settings, with the Automatic Sites and Automatic Comments fieldsets in their default state." title="The Deploy Previews settings" type="screenshot" />

<comp.Notice info_type="important">
  Enabling the *Delete automatically created Sites when Pull Requests are closed or merged* setting is a destructive action, which will remove build history, stable domain, and any custom authentication for all future *Automatic Sites* in the *Project*.
  
  If you need to preserve an automatically created *Site* after its *Pull Request* closes, leave this option off and delete *Sites* manually from the *Project Sites Browser* when you are ready.
</comp.Notice>

Turning these automations on or off does not retroactively process historical *Pull Requests*, with one exception: disabling *Automatic Comments* or changing the *Comment footer* runs a single reconciliation across every open *Pull Request*. For more information, please read our documentation on [enabling and disabling Deploy Preview automations](/documentation/developer-articles/what-are-deploy-previews/#enabling-and-disabling-deploy-preview-automations).

<comp.Notice info_type="info">
  If you do not see the *Deploy Previews* section under *Project Settings*, your *Organization* may not have *Deploy Previews* enabled on its plan, or you may not have the `project:settings:deploy-previews:read` permission. Contact our support team or an Owner in your *Organization* to check.
</comp.Notice>

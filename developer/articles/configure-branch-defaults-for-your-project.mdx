<comp.Notice info_type="permissions">
  **Permissions required**

  Members of the Owners and Developers [Default Permission Groups](/documentation/user-articles/what-are-default-permission-groups/), or [Custom Permission Groups](/documentation/developer-articles/what-are-custom-permission-groups/) with the `project:settings:details:write` permission, can configure *Branch Defaults* for a *Project*.
</comp.Notice>

*Branch Defaults* control how CloudCannon configures new branched <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/s/site.yml">Sites</comp.GlossaryTerm> in your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/p/project.yml">Project</comp.GlossaryTerm>. Before configuring them, you may want to read our documentation on [what Branch Defaults are](/documentation/developer-articles/what-are-branch-defaults/) and [adding a Site to your Project](/documentation/developer-articles/add-a-site-to-your-project/).

## Open the Branch Defaults section

To reach the *Branch Defaults* form:

1. Click *Projects* in the *App Sidebar*. CloudCannon will open the *Projects Browser*.
2. Select the *Project* you want to configure. CloudCannon will open the *Project Sites Browser*.
3. Click the *Project Settings* tab.
4. Select *Branch Defaults* from the *Section Navigation*.

## Configure the Publish settings

The *Publish settings* fieldset controls how branched *Sites* publish back to their parent *Site*.

1. Under *Default Publish mode*, select one of the radio options:
    * *Inherit from branched site*
    * *Merge changes immediately*
    * *Create a Pull Request for approval*
2. Under *After publish complete*, switch the *Delete branched site after publishing* checkbox on or off.
3. Click the *Update Branch Defaults* button.

For more information on what each option does, please read our documentation on [Branch Defaults](/documentation/developer-articles/what-are-branch-defaults/#publish-settings).

## Configure the Authentication settings

The *Authentication settings* fieldset controls whether new branched *Sites* require a password or a CloudCannon login.

1. Under *Default authentication method*, select *None*, *Password*, or *CloudCannon*.
2. If you selected *Password*, enter a password in the *Default site password* field. New branched *Sites* will use this password until you change it.
3. Click the *Update Branch Defaults* button.

## Configure the Permission settings

<comp.Notice info_type="info">
  The *Permission settings* fieldset is only visible when your *Organization* has [Custom Permission Groups](/documentation/developer-articles/what-are-custom-permission-groups/) enabled.
</comp.Notice>

The *Permission settings* fieldset controls whether CloudCannon grants new permissions to a *Custom Permission Group* when one of its members branches a *Site*.

1. Switch the *Do not grant new permissions on Site branch* checkbox on or off.
    * *Off* (default) — When a team member in a *Custom Permission Group* branches a *Site*, CloudCannon grants their group permissions on the new branched *Site*.
    * *On* — CloudCannon does not grant new permissions; team members rely on the permissions you have already defined.
2. Click the *Update Branch Defaults* button.

CloudCannon will apply your updated *Branch Defaults* to *Sites* created after the change. Existing *Sites* keep their current settings.

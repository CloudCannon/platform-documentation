This guide is for teams migrating from CloudCannon's deprecated built-in i18n feature to <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/r/rosey.yml">Rosey</comp.GlossaryTerm>. If you are adding <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/i/internationalization.yml">internationalization</comp.GlossaryTerm> to your <comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/s/site.yml">Site</comp.GlossaryTerm> for the first time without a prior i18n setup, see the [Add internationalization with Rosey](/documentation/developer-guides/add-internationalization-with-rosey/) guide instead.

CloudCannon's built-in i18n feature translated your site at build time. You enabled it in your site's *Build* settings using the *Use I18n* checkbox and set a *Default Locale* for the base language. You tagged translatable content in your HTML templates with `data-i18n` attributes and stored translation files in a `_locales` directory. CloudCannon built a translated version of your site for each locale and routed visitors based on their language settings.

<comp.Notice info_type="info">
  You can continue using CloudCannon's built-in i18n feature, but it is no longer actively supported and will not receive updates. CloudCannon recommends migrating to Rosey.
</comp.Notice>

<comp.GlossaryTerm term="/user/glossary/r/rosey.yml">Rosey</comp.GlossaryTerm> is an open-source i18n tool for static sites, developed and maintained by CloudCannon. It works with any static site generator and supports translating text content, images, and HTML attributes such as `alt` text and `meta` descriptions. Rosey also tracks stale translations, flagging strings that need updating when your source content changes, and you can test your translated site locally before pushing to CloudCannon.

In this guide, we'll walk through migrating your site to Rosey. You'll rename your locale directory, convert your translation files to JSON, and retag your HTML elements. Your existing translation content carries over.

Before we begin, this guide assumes:

- Your site uses CloudCannon's built-in i18n feature, with `data-i18n` attributes in your HTML templates and translation files in a `_locales` directory.
- You have separate staging and production branches — Rosey runs only on your production branch to keep the *Visual Editor* available for content editing on your staging branch.
- You have NPM available, or can initialize it on your project with `npm init`.

## Visual Editor caveat

Rosey moves your output files during the build process, which can interfere with the *Visual Editor*. CloudCannon needs your output files in a predictable location to load them for editing. The recommended approach is to run Rosey only on your production branch, not on your staging (editing) branch.

<comp.Notice info_type="info">
  This guide assumes you are using a workflow with separate staging and production branches. See the [staging workflow guide](/documentation/developer-guides/staging-workflow-guide/) for guidance on setting this up.
</comp.Notice>

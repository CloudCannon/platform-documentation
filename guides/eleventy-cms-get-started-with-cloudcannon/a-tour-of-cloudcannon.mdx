Let’s go through a brief tour of CloudCannon to help you get the most
value out of it.

## Dashboard

The dashboard gives an overview of your site and recent activity.

<comp.DocsImage path="https://dam-cdn.cloudcannon.com/cloudcannon-eleventy-tour-dashboard.jpg" alt="CloudCannon dashboard" type="screenshot" />

## Usage

Usage indicates the current bandwidth and build time utilization.

<comp.DocsImage path="https://dam-cdn.cloudcannon.com/cloudcannon-eleventy-tour-usage.jpg" alt="CloudCannon usage dashboard" type="screenshot" />

## Pages

Lists the pages on your site. Clicking on a page will open it in an
editing interface.

<comp.DocsImage path="https://dam-cdn.cloudcannon.com/cloudcannon-eleventy-tour-pages.jpg" alt="CloudCannon pages dashboard" type="screenshot" />

## Collections


Your collections will also list in the sidebar. In this case, the site has
a posts tag for the blog.

<comp.DocsImage path="https://dam-cdn.cloudcannon.com/cloudcannon-eleventy-tour-collections.jpg" alt="CloudCannon collections dashboard" type="screenshot" />

## Data

All your data files will be available in the data section.

<comp.DocsImage path="https://dam-cdn.cloudcannon.com/cloudcannon-eleventy-tour-data.jpg" alt="CloudCannon data dashboard" type="screenshot" />

## Everything else

Everything we’ve been through so far is accessible for your editing team.
The rest of the items are visible for developers only. We have:

* **Status** — Show the current status of your Git sync and build output.

* **Files** — A browser showing all the source files on the site.

* **Reports** — Lists the build information, syncs, and CloudCannon
activity on your site.

* **Settings** — Configure your build setting, hosting, sharing and other
site settings.
